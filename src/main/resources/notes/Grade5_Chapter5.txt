5. Ratio & Percentage – Exploring Ratios and Percentages
Understanding Ratios
A ratio compares two quantities.
Example: If a class has 10 boys and 15 girls, the ratio of boys to girls is 10:15, which simplifies to 2:3.
Understanding Percentages
Percentage means "per hundred".
Formula: (Part/Whole) × 100
Example: If a student scores 45 out of 50,
Percentage = (45/50) × 100 = 90%
Converting Between Fractions, Decimals, and Percentages
Fraction to Percentage: Multiply by 100
Example: 3/4 → (3/4) × 100 = 75%
Decimal to Percentage: Move decimal two places right
Example: 0.85 = 85%
Percentage to Decimal: Move decimal two places left
Example: 75% = 0.75
Final Tips for Mastering Class 5 Mathematics:
Practice daily – Math gets easier with regular problem-solving.
Memorize key formulas – Helps solve problems faster.
Understand concepts – Don’t just memorize, understand why formulas work.
Check your work – Always double-check calculations to avoid mistakes.
