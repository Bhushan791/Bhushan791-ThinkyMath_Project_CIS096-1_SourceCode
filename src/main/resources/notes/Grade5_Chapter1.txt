1. Number System – Understanding Number Operations
Place Value & Face Value
Place Value refers to the value of a digit depending on its position in a number.
Example: In 4,527, the place value of 4 is 4,000, 5 is 500, etc.
Face Value is the digit itself, regardless of its position.
Example: In 4,527, the face value of 5 is simply 5.
Types of Numbers
Natural Numbers: 1, 2, 3, 4, … (Counting numbers)
Whole Numbers: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, … (Natural numbers including 0)
Even Numbers: Numbers divisible by 2 (e.g., 2, 4, 6, 8, …)
Odd Numbers: Numbers not divisible by 2 (e.g., 1, 3, 5, 7, …)
Prime Numbers: Numbers having only two factors (1 and itself), e.g., 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, …
Composite Numbers: Numbers having more than two factors, e.g., 4, 6, 8, 9, 10, …
